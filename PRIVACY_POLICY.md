# Privacy Policy - NeoLink EHR System

**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Effective Date:** February 2, 2026

## 1. Introduction
NeoLink is an Electronic Health Record (EHR) system designed for government hospitals in India, specifically for NICU and PICU units. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your digital personal data in compliance with the **Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023**.

## 2. Information We Collect
We collect data from healthcare professionals (Data Principals) and patients (via Data Principals):
- **For Healthcare Professionals**: Display name, UserID, email, professional role, and hospital/institution affiliation.
- **For Patients**: Name, age, gender, medical history, clinical observations, diagnoses, and treatment records.

### 2.1 Third-Party Data Processors
We use the following third-party services for AI-powered clinical assistance:
- **OpenAI (GPT Models)**: For clinical note generation, differential diagnosis, medication dosing recommendations, and clinical decision support.
- **Google Gemini AI**: For clinical insights, risk prediction, and medical analytics.
- **Data Location**: These services process data on servers located **outside India** (primarily USA).
- **Data Protection**: All patient data sent to these processors is **de-identified** (patient names are anonymized/pseudonymized). We have executed Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) with these providers requiring GDPR-equivalent protection.

> [!WARNING]
> **AI Features Consent Required**: By using AI-powered features in NeoLink, you explicitly consent to your de-identified health data being processed by OpenAI and Google Gemini under strict contractual data protection obligations. You may opt out of AI features without affecting core EHR functionality.

## 3. Purpose of Processing (Section 4 of DPDP Act)
The personal data is processed solely for:
1. Providing neonatal and pediatric healthcare services.
2. Clinical documentation and medical record keeping.
3. Healthcare analytics for hospital administration.
4. Referral and transfer of patients between government institutions.
5. **Legitimate Use**: Processing by the State or its instrumentalities for providing subsidies, benefits, certificates, licenses, or permits (Section 7(b)).

## 4. Consent (Section 6 of DPDP Act)
By using NeoLink, you provide explicit, informed, and unambiguous consent for the processing of your data for the purposes stated above. You have the right to withdraw consent at any time, although this may affect your ability to use the system.

## 5. Your Rights
Under the DPDP Act 2023, you have the following rights:
- **Right to Access**: Request a summary of your personal data and processing activities.
- **Right to Correction and Erasure**: Request correction of inaccurate data or deletion of data no longer required.
- **Right to Grievance Redressal**: Register a complaint with the Data Fiduciary.
- **Right to Nominate**: Nominate an individual to exercise your rights in the event of death or incapacity.

## 6. Data Security (Section 8 of DPDP Act)
We implement robust technical and organizational measures to prevent personal data breaches, including:
- 256-bit SSL encryption.
- Role-based access control (RBAC).
- Comprehensive audit logging of all data modifications.

## 7. Data Residency
All personal data is stored on secure servers located within the **Republic of India (Mumbai Region)**. No personal data is transferred outside the territory of India.

## 8. Data Breach Notification
In the event of a personal data breach, NeoLink will notify the **Data Protection Board of India** and the affected individuals **within 72 hours** as required by Section 8(6) of the DPDP Act 2023.

## 9. Parental Consent for Minors (Section 9 Compliance)
As a NICU/PICU EHR system, NeoLink processes health data of **children** (minors under 18 years). Under Section 9 of the DPDP Act:
- **Parental consent is obtained** via a **physical consent form** signed by parents/legal guardians during patient admission.
- Hospital staff verify and digitally record the consent in the system.
- Verification method: Physical signature + Staff attestation.
- Parents retain all Data Principal rights (access, correction, erasure) on behalf of their child.

## 10. Data Retention
- **Active Patient Records**: Retained for the duration of treatment + **5 years** (as per Medical Council of India guidelines).
- **Discharged/Deceased Patient Records**: Retained for **5 years** for legal and audit purposes, then archived or anonymized.
- **User Account Data**: Retained while account is active + **1 year** after deactivation, then permanently deleted.
- **AI Processing Logs**: Retained for **90 days** for quality assurance, then deleted.
